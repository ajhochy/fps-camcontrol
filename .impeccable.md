## Design Context

### Users
AV technician / camera operator running this during a live production event. Low-light booth environment, multiple screens, high pressure. Needs information at a glance — no hunting.

### Brand Personality
Tactical, broadcast-grade, purpose-built. This is a tool that serious people use in serious situations. It should feel like it belongs next to an ATEM switcher and a Ross Video router, not like a developer dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark — production booths are always dark. Color palette draws from broadcast equipment hardware: deep charcoal backgrounds with a slight cool tint, amber as the primary accent (hardware status LED color), precise status colors for program/preview/connections. Typography draws from panel labeling on professional broadcast equipment: tight condensed sans-serif for labels, legible grotesque for data. No rounded pill shapes. No glassmorphism. No gradient text. Corners are sharp or nearly so.

Reference: Blackmagic ATEM Software Control, Ross Video Dashboard, hardware video switcher front panels.
Anti-reference: Generic SaaS dashboards, developer dark-mode tools, anything that looks like Tailwind defaults.

### Design Principles
1. **Information density over whitespace** — this is a production tool, not a marketing page
2. **Status at a glance** — critical state (live, preview, connections) must be immediately readable
3. **Every element feels intentional** — nothing decorative that doesn't earn its place
4. **Hardware aesthetic** — it should look like firmware UI for professional equipment
5. **Calm under pressure** — nothing flashing or demanding attention except real alerts
